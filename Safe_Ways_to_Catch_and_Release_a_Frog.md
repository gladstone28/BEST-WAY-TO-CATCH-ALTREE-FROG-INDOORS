This guide provides **two safe, humane, and effective methods** for catching and releasing a frog that has entered a house.  
Both methods are suitable for use in Jamaica and prioritize **human safety and animal welfare**.

---

## Method 1: Container & Cardboard Method (General Frog Method)

### Best for:
- Frogs on the **floor**
- Smaller or less active frogs
- Situations with limited climbing surfaces

### What You‚Äôll Need:
- A **bucket, bowl, or plastic container**
- **Cardboard, magazine, or stiff paper**
- Optional: **gloves or towel**

### Steps:
1. Stay calm and move slowly to avoid startling the frog.
2. Gently guide the frog toward a wall or corner.
3. Place the container over the frog in one smooth motion.
4. Slide the cardboard underneath the container, trapping the frog inside.
5. Hold the container and cardboard together firmly.
6. Carry it outside and gently release the frog onto grass or soil.

### Important Notes:
- Do **not** use chemicals, salt, or sprays.
- Wash hands after handling.
- Release the frog away from hot concrete surfaces.

## Method 2: Towel & Deep Bucket Method (Large Tree Frog ‚Äì Recommended)

### Best for:
- **Large tree frogs**
- Frogs on **walls, curtains, or furniture**
- Strong jumpers and climbers

### What You‚Äôll Need:
- A **deep bucket or tall container**
- A **towel or small sheet**
- **Cardboard** to cover the container
- Optional: **gloves**

### Steps:
1. Dim or turn off bright lights to calm the frog.
2. Gently throw the towel over the frog ‚Äî it will freeze.
3. Cup the frog through the towel using both hands (do not squeeze).
4. Lower the towel and frog into the deep bucket.
5. Quickly remove the towel and cover the bucket with cardboard.
6. Take the frog outside and release it near bushes or trees.

### Important Notes:
- Use a **deep container** to prevent escape.
- Avoid bare-hand grabbing if possible.
- Never hit or chase the frog.

---

## What NOT to Do
- Do not stomp, hit, or injure the frog.
- Do not use insect spray or chemicals.
- Do not release frogs indoors or on hot pavement.

---

## Why This Matters
Frogs are harmless, protected wildlife and play a vital role in controlling insects.  
Relocating them safely benefits both people and the environment.
